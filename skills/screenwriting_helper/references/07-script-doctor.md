# 07 — Script Doctor: Diagnostic Mode

When analyzing a script, outline, or story concept, run through these diagnostic layers in order. Foundational issues (Layer 1-2) must be addressed before craft issues (Layer 3-6).

## Layer 1: Story Essentials (Fatal if Missing)

Check each. Any absence is a fundamental structural problem:

| Essential | Diagnostic Question | Severity |
|-----------|-------------------|----------|
| CHARACTER | Is there an empathetic, proactive MC? | CRITICAL |
| GOAL | Does the MC have a clear, strong main goal? | CRITICAL |
| STAKES | Are there meaningful consequences for success AND failure? | CRITICAL |
| CLOCK | Is there urgency — a reason to act now? | HIGH |
| OBSTACLE | Is there a formidable opposing force? | CRITICAL |
| ENDING | Does the story resolve its central dramatic question? | CRITICAL |
| THEME | Is there a philosophical argument underlying the plot? | HIGH |
| CHANGE | Is something meaningfully different by the end? | CRITICAL |

## Layer 2: Character Health Check

| Check | Diagnostic Question | Severity |
|-------|-------------------|----------|
| MC Empathy | Does MC have likeable traits + victimization + virtue + desirable qualities? | HIGH |
| MC Proactivity | Does MC drive the story through decisions (not just react to events)? | HIGH |
| Want vs Need | Are these defined and do they come into conflict? | MEDIUM |
| Flaw | Does MC have a psychological flaw connected to the theme? | HIGH |
| Arc | Does MC undergo one of the 5 arc types? | HIGH |
| Antagonist Goal | Does the antagonist have a goal that directly conflicts with MC's? | HIGH |
| Antagonist Calibration | Is the villain's moral complexity appropriate for the story's tone? | MEDIUM |
| Web of Conflict | Does every character pairing have some source of friction? | MEDIUM |
| Character Complexity | Are there contradictions in major characters (not just coherent trait bundles)? | LOW |
| Revealing Character | Are traits shown through action/decision, not stated directly? | MEDIUM |

## Layer 3: Theme Integration

| Check | Diagnostic Question | Severity |
|-------|-------------------|----------|
| Theme Defined | Can the theme be expressed as a yes/no question, statement, or X vs Y? | HIGH |
| Truth/Lie Assigned | Is one side the Truth and one the Lie? | MEDIUM |
| Plot Reflects Theme | Do major dilemmas force choices between Truth and Lie? | HIGH |
| Arc Reflects Theme | Does the MC's arc track with their journey from Lie to Truth (or vice versa)? | HIGH |
| Static Characters | Do supporting characters represent different positions on the theme? | MEDIUM |
| Theme Not Preached | Is the theme shown through action/consequence, never stated in speeches? | MEDIUM |

## Layer 4: Structure & Scene Audit

| Check | Diagnostic Question | Severity |
|-------|-------------------|----------|
| Three Acts | Does the story have a clear beginning, middle, and end? | HIGH |
| Inciting Incident | Is the status quo disrupted by ~12.5% of the story? | MEDIUM |
| Midpoint Turn | Is there a major turning point near the midpoint? | MEDIUM |
| Scene-Sequel | Does every scene follow Goal→Conflict→Disaster or Reaction→Dilemma→Decision? | HIGH |
| Conflict in Every Scene | Does every single scene have conflict (internal or external)? | HIGH |
| Change in Every Scene | Has something changed by the end of every scene? | HIGH |
| Cuttable Scenes | Are there scenes that don't serve plot, character, or theme? | MEDIUM |
| Emotional Rollercoaster | Do success and failure alternate? Do all plotlines sync at major beats? | MEDIUM |

## Layer 5: Dialogue & Scene Craft

| Check | Diagnostic Question | Severity |
|-------|-------------------|----------|
| Conflicting Agendas | Does every dialogue scene have characters with opposing goals? | HIGH |
| Stakes in Dialogue | Is something on the line in each conversation? | MEDIUM |
| The Filter | Are characters speaking through a filter (not stating goals directly)? | MEDIUM |
| The Button | Does every scene end on its strongest line? | MEDIUM |
| Exposition Wrapped | Is exposition delivered through conflict, not info dumps? | MEDIUM |
| Show Don't Tell | Are character traits/emotions shown through action, not stated? | MEDIUM |
| On-the-Nose Detection | Are characters saying exactly what they feel/mean? (Bad sign) | MEDIUM |
| Comedy Check | If comedy is present, does it follow Benign Violation Theory? | LOW |

## Layer 6: Tension & Payoff Audit

| Check | Diagnostic Question | Severity |
|-------|-------------------|----------|
| Info-States | Are info-state transitions used deliberately for emotional impact? | MEDIUM |
| Open Questions | Are questions raised that keep the audience intrigued? | MEDIUM |
| Drip-Feed Answers | Are answers delivered gradually (not all at once)? | MEDIUM |
| Setup/Payoff Chains | Does every setup have a payoff? Does every payoff have a setup? | HIGH |
| Convergence at Ending | Do multiple payoffs converge at the climax/denouement? | MEDIUM |
| Mystery Answers | Are reveals unexpected + fitting + explanatory + consequential? | MEDIUM |

## Layer 7: Cliché & Anti-Pattern Detection

| Anti-Pattern | Description | Severity |
|-------------|-------------|----------|
| Forced Crime/Violence | Guns, drugs, cursing forced into the story without narrative purpose | MEDIUM |
| Preaching Theme | Theme directly stated in dialogue or narrated to audience | HIGH |
| Mary Sue | MC has no meaningful flaws, is universally loved, never fails | HIGH |
| Saving the World | Stakes are so large they become emotionally empty | MEDIUM |
| Bad Romance | Romance told through long glances instead of connection; no healthy relationships last | MEDIUM |
| Deus Ex Machina | Character saved at last minute by coincidence, or "dies" then revives | HIGH |
| Plotless Character Study | "Character-driven" used as excuse for no plot | HIGH |
| False Death | Character appears dead but is revived — trying to have cake and eat it | MEDIUM |

## Layer 8: Diversity & Representation Check

| Check | Diagnostic Question | Severity |
|-------|-------------------|----------|
| Bechdel Test | Two+ named female characters who talk to each other about something other than a man? | LOW |
| Reverse Bechdel | Same test applied to male characters? | LOW |
| Full Characters | Are characters from marginalized groups given flaws, arcs, and agency (not just tokenized)? | MEDIUM |
| Cultural Research | If depicting another culture, is it well-researched and nuanced? | MEDIUM |

---

## Revision Protocol

After diagnosis, recommend these revision practices:

1. Address CRITICAL issues first (missing story essentials, broken structure)
2. Then HIGH issues (weak empathy, missing theme integration, dead scenes)
3. Then MEDIUM/LOW (polish-level craft improvements)

For each issue found:
- Name the specific problem
- Explain why it matters (what the audience will experience)
- Suggest 2-3 possible fixes (options, not mandates)
- Reference which framework/technique addresses it

Additional revision practices:
- Read all dialogue aloud — catch stiffness, unnaturalness, on-the-nose lines
- For every scene, ask: "What has changed?" If nothing, cut or rework
- For every scene, ask: "Does this serve plot, character, or theme?" If not, cut
- Kill darlings — cut beloved elements that don't serve the story

---

## Claude Behavior in Diagnostic Mode

- Run through all 8 layers systematically
- Output a structured diagnostic report as a .md file
- Use severity levels to prioritize recommendations
- For each issue: name it, explain impact, suggest 2-3 fixes
- Be honest about problems but constructive — focus on solutions
- Don't overwhelm: if there are many issues, group them and prioritize
- Re-run relevant layers after the user makes changes
